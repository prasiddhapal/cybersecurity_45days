# 📌 Objective

The objective of this task is to analyze phishing email samples to identify phishing characteristics, email spoofing techniques, suspicious links, social engineering tactics, and email header anomalies. This analysis helps understand how phishing attacks work and how they can be detected and prevented.

---

# 🎯 Project Overview

Phishing is one of the most common cyberattacks used to steal sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, banking details, and personal information. Attackers often impersonate trusted organizations and use deceptive emails to trick users into clicking malicious links or downloading harmful attachments.

In this project, two phishing email samples were analyzed to identify Indicators of Compromise (IOCs), email spoofing techniques, suspicious URLs, and social engineering tactics.

# 🔍 Methodology

The following steps were followed during the investigation:

1. Obtained phishing email samples.
2. Examined sender email addresses.
3. Inspected email headers.
4. Verified SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records.
5. Identified suspicious links and domains.
6. Checked attachments (if present).
7. Analyzed social engineering techniques.
8. Documented Indicators of Compromise (IOCs).
9. Prepared investigation reports.
10. Suggested mitigation and security recommendations.

# 🛡️ Best Security Practices

- Verify sender email addresses.
- Hover over links before clicking.
- Avoid downloading unexpected attachments.
- Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).
- Keep antivirus software updated.
- Report suspicious emails immediately.
- Verify requests for sensitive information.
